# Four-island prospective field validation

## Purpose

This trip is the prospective external test of ACSP, not another retrospective GBIF benchmark. The predeclared islands are Izu Oshima, Toshima, Niijima, and Kozushima, with one field day per island.

## Minimum design

Before visiting any site, freeze one export containing three sites per island:

1. the highest-ranked integrated ACSP site;
2. the highest-ranked ACSP site that adds geographic or environmental complementarity;
3. one accessible matched-control site drawn from the same island and broad habitat/access class but outside the ACSP Top-k.

If time safely permits, add a fourth exploratory divergence site where local habitat and macro SDM support disagree. Do not replace failed, inaccessible, or negative sites after seeing the field result. Record them as planned outcomes.

## Standardized observation

Use the same nominal search radius and target search time at every site. A practical starting protocol is a 100 m search radius and 30 minutes of active searching, adjusted only for safety or legal access. Record the actual effort.

Required fields:

- island, site ID, frozen priority rank, candidate role, and coordinates;
- arrival and departure time, observer, weather, and access mode;
- searched radius or track, minutes searched, and inaccessible fraction;
- target detected, not detected, uncertain identification, or not surveyable;
- abundance, flowering status, habitat notes, photographs, specimen/DNA collection, and GPS accuracy;
- whether the population was previously known to the observer.

`Not detected` is not confirmed absence. `Not surveyable` must remain separate from biological non-detection.

## Primary comparison

Primary endpoint: target detection within the standardized effort. Report ACSP versus matched-control detection counts and exact uncertainty, with island retained as a blocking factor. Secondary endpoints are time to first detection, abundance, access failure, environmental coverage, and newly confirmed populations.

This four-island trip is a valuable pilot external validation, but 12-16 sites cannot establish universal weights. Keep the current production weights unless this trip is later combined with additional taxa, observers, seasons, and regions.

## Interpretation guardrails

- Macro SDM is a broad relative-suitability filter, not a calibrated occupancy probability.
- A successful known-location revisit does not validate model-only discovery.
- A negative visit may reflect occupancy, detectability, phenology, access, or search effort.
- Freeze site roles and analysis rules before the first survey to prevent favorable post-hoc relabeling.
